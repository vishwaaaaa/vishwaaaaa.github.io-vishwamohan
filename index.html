December 15, 2023 University of Michigan

High-fidelity RANS CFD based Aerodynamic shape optimization

Fluid flow can be tricky to simulate either in terms of the accuracy of the simulations or the time taken to obtain the results. Performing an aircraft optimization considering the flow considering all the flow characteristics becomes a tough problem to formulate and setup. The choice of wrong CFD solver and turbulence model might lead to the unconverged solution, especially when dealing with operating points of Mach = 0.8 (cruise condition for commerical aircrafts). I perfomed a the shape optimization of a Boeing A727 200 Adv wing with the objective of minimizing fuel burnt with the constraint of range greater than that achieved by the actual aircraft. To capture the correct flow features (shocks, separation, etc..), ADflow CFD solver was used in this project. It's a structured multiblock solver with overset meshes. with discrete adjoints(for gradients) and MPI (for parallelization). The initial geometry is design in OpenVSP using the available pictures and literature on A727 200 Adv. The surface meshing is done in Pointewise. The volume mesh was generated using pyHyp. This is fed to the IPOPT optimization to obtain the final optimized geometry

November 15, 2023 University of Michigan

Finite Element Analysis of thin Polygonal shells

Thin walled shells are notorious for having unpredictable collapse load. Further they show a great imperfection sensitivity, which means in the presence of imperfection they collapse drastically. In this research work, we try to design shells so that they can make use of the post-buckling capacity of plates. We are able to achieve a higher load carrying capacity and shells that are insensitive to imperfections.

April 23, 2023 University of Michigan

Eular Equation CFD solver for transonic flows

The present project is an extension of the finite-volume method (FVM) solver of the incompressible Euler equations, to implement an implicit time marching scheme to reduce the run time of the solver. An implicit backward Euler time integration with a Newton solver is imple mented in the first and second-order FVM solvers. The solutions from the first-order FVM solver with the implicit scheme opti mized for CFL number are compared with those obtained from the solver with the ex plicit scheme. For the second-order FVM solver, an implicit scheme with the approx imate Jacobian developed for the first-order FVM as well as an implicit scheme with frozen limiter Jacobian are implemented. The solutions obtained from them are com pared with those obtained from the solver with the explicit scheme. A significant re duction in wall clock time is achieved for implicit time-marching schemes with opti mized CFL numbers compared to explicit schemes for both orders of FVM solver while giving approximately the same re sults

10 Dec, 2021 IIT Bombay, India

Study of Plume driven by temperature gradients

The temperature gradient-driven flow, also known as thermogravitational convection or thermally induced buoyancy-driven flow, is a phenomenon where fluid motion is generated in response to temperature differences within a fluid. When a fluid experiences non-uniform heating or cooling, it leads to variations in temperature across different regions. As a result, the fluid density also varies due to thermal expansion or contraction. This density difference causes buoyancy forces, leading to fluid motion. In the context of natural convection, warmer fluid near a heat source tends to rise, while cooler fluid descends, creating a circulation pattern This study was done with the intension of developing a concentration driven flow and crystalization.

Magnetohydrodynamic 3D channel flows using OpenFOAM

This project is aimed to study numerically the phenomena of Magneto-hydrodynamics (MHD). The focus of the paper is narrowed down to understanding a particular case in the field of MHD, i.e, low magnetic Reynolds number Rm. Here one the most popular open source CFD software OpenFOAM is employed to set up the problem. The two major for- mulation of MHD equations are discussed in the form of mhdFoam and epotFoam, and further the most appropri- ate method is used to simulate the square duct flow for low Rm number. Based on the boundary conditions the two most popular MHD flows namely hunt and shercliff cases are stud- ied.

April 22, 2022 NITK

Fluid Structure Interaction Study in Carotid Artery and Qualitative Analysis of Diseased Artery

The carotid arteries are a pair of blood vessels located on both sides of the human neck that deliver blood to the brain and head. Carotid artery disease occurs when fatty deposits ( plague ) clog the blood vessels. As a consequence of this deposition, the severe narrowing of arteries occurs. This narrowing could block the blood flow, causing a stroke. In the present work, we analyze the blood through the bifurcation of the Carotid Artery. The analysis is done by adopting the Fluid-Structure Interaction (FSI ) modeling method. FSI is a Multi-physics coupling between the laws that describe fluid dynamics and structural mechanics.
